What we do

Abstract

There have been many attempts to identify relations among concepts corresponding to terms from Unified Medical Language System (UMLS). One promising category of attempts is relatedness measures and a representative of these measures is the vector method which is based on UMLS definition texts. However, there are two limitations to the method: (1) narrow coverage, (2) concerns about performance. Therefore, we propose a concept embedding model for UMLS semantic relatedness measure to overcome the limitations. We generated information not in UMLS for wide coverage and utilized state-of-the-art embedding methods to obtain performance for relatedness measure. As a result, we validated that our method provides approximately 4.77% higher coverage and 0.159 performance improvement over the vector method. Furthermore, we believe that our method is not limited to UMLS and may be used to calculate various other biomedical terms’ similarity.

Materials and Methods

We propose a method which solves the coverage limitations of conventional vector-based UMLS relatedness measures and shows improved performance.

Extension of definition of UMLS CUI for feature vector extraction

UMLS database contains CUI concept definition information for only a small portion of CUI concepts, limiting the potential coverage of relatedness measures. In the case of UMLS2015, only 162,973 CUI concepts (6.5% of CUI concepts) contain definition information. We have used two methods to expand CUI definition texts that UMLS database does not offer.

Model generation with a document embedding method for UMLS CUI feature vector generation

We analyzed the relationship between a CUI and a definition of the CUI and concluded that it is a label relationship, not an inclusive relationship. We found that CUI is a label and a definition of CUI is a feature. Then we utilized a previous method which is based on state-of-the-art word and document embedding techniques.
